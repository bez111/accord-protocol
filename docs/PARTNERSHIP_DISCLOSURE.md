# Partnership and Disclosure Policy

Accord may receive inbound offers for hardware-wallet reviews, affiliate links,
discount codes, integrations, sponsored content, or ecosystem co-marketing.
Those offers can be useful, but they must not dilute Accord's security posture.

This policy applies to public posts, reviews, launch materials, partner pages,
and any content that could reasonably influence a developer's or user's
security decisions.

## Non-negotiables

- Reviews and technical posts stay independent. No scripts, approval rights, or
  guaranteed positive coverage.
- Compensation, free devices, referral links, discounts, or affiliate terms must
  be clearly disclosed in the same content where the partner is mentioned.
- Partner content must not imply that Accord is mainnet-certified while the
  manifests remain default-deny.
- No partner may be described as "Accord-certified", "officially audited by
  Accord", or "safe for real funds" unless a separate public certification
  process exists and says exactly that.
- Review devices and wallets should use fresh test keys. Do not import
  production seed phrases into review hardware or partner apps.
- Public content should distinguish product review, integration feasibility,
  and protocol certification. They are different claims.

## Hardware wallet review rules

Hardware wallet collaborations are especially sensitive because they touch key
management and buyer authorization.

Before accepting a hardware wallet review, collect:

- exact device model and firmware line;
- official website and developer documentation links;
- EVM/Base support, WalletConnect support, typed-data signing support, and
  transaction-decoding capabilities;
- public security model, audit reports, reproducible-build claims, and secure
  element claims;
- whether the device is free with no obligation to publish;
- affiliate/referral payout terms and discount-code requirements;
- any claims the vendor expects to appear in content.

Allowed content angle:

```text
Can a hardware wallet make agent-payment authorization safer?
```

Avoid content angle:

```text
This wallet makes Accord production-safe.
```

## Affiliate and referral rules

Affiliate links are allowed only when:

- the content is still useful without the affiliate link;
- the disclosure is prominent;
- the review remains free to criticize the product;
- security caveats are preserved;
- the audience is not pushed toward real-fund Accord workflows before mainnet
  certification.

Template disclosure:

```text
Disclosure: the vendor provided a review device and may pay a referral
commission if you buy through the link. The review is independent, the vendor
did not approve the content, and Accord remains testnet-first / not
mainnet-certified.
```

## Default inbound reply

```text
Thanks for reaching out.

We are open to technical collaborations, but Accord reviews stay independent:
no scripts, no approval rights, no guaranteed positive coverage, and clear
disclosure of any free device, discount, referral or affiliate terms.

Accord is currently alpha / testnet-first and not mainnet-certified, so any
content would focus on security, developer integration, and buyer authorization
UX rather than real-fund production recommendations.

Please send the exact device/model or integration scope, public developer docs,
security/audit materials, and the full commercial terms.
```

## Launch interaction

Public launch posts should prioritize Accord's protocol evidence: schemas,
receipts, conformance, public pilots, status, and security posture. Partner
content should not be mixed into the initial launch unless it directly improves
developer understanding and follows this policy.
